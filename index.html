Watering schedule — Mon / Wed / Fri baseline
Fixed weekly schedule: non-consecutive days allow soil to absorb and foliage to dry between sessions (reduces brown patch risk on tall fescue in VA clay). In extreme heat (100°F+ forecast), Sunday auto-suggests as a 4th day.
Controller program settings — split-pulse cycle-and-soak
Your controller runs two programs with an asymmetric split: Program 1 is a light pre-wet pulse (45% of total runtime) that primes the clay for infiltration without runoff. Program 2 is the heavier main pulse (55%) that delivers the deep water into already-moistened soil. Start times are fixed; only the runtime scales with today's %.
Static start times: P1 = 2:30 AM, P2 = 4:30 AM. These never change — only the per-program runtime (and therefore soak gap) shifts with today's %. P2 finishes before 6 AM even at extreme-heat 120%, so the household shower window is clear from 6 AM on every water day.
Zone-by-zone — what to enter in each program
Each zone's 100% summer baseline is split 45% into Program 1 (pre-wet pulse) and 55% into Program 2 (main pulse). The values below show exactly what minutes to enter for each zone in each program at today's tier. The global % you set on the controller scales everything proportionally.

Tier logic — weather-driven % scaling
Your 100% baselines are already calibrated for your soil and slope realities — these are the peak-summer targets. The tiered scaling below adjusts UP or DOWN from those known-good numbers based on the forecast high for each watering day. Logic follows Virginia Cooperative Extension guidance for tall fescue and ET-based (evapotranspiration) adjustment principles used in smart irrigation controllers.

Standards validation — pachysandra establishment phase
✓
Pamphlet supersedes general internet sources for establishment. The installer pamphlet specifies 2–3× per week during establishment — more aggressive than the generic "weekly" guidance found in most online sources. This is correct and appropriate for the critical 6–8 week transplant shock window. Previous dashboard used weekly frequency (Almanac, NC State general guidance). Pamphlet is governing authority for these specific plantings and has been applied.
✓
Pamphlet frequency (2–3×/week) is consistent with ISA transplant shock protocols. The 2–3× per week establishment rate aligns with VA DOF guidance for newly planted trees and the ISA standard for preventing transplant shock — it is not excessive. Source: VA DOF — "newly planted trees should be watered 2–3 times per week"; consistent with pamphlet.
✓
1" of water per week target is the professional standard. The pamphlet's 1"/week rule is the accepted baseline used by professional landscapers, cooperative extensions, and the ISA. At a typical soaker rate of ~0.5–1 gph, 15–20 min sessions 2–3× per week delivers approximately this target across the root zone. Source: Plant Me Green; VA DOF; ISA — all cite 1"/week as the standard new-planting target.
✓
Phase 2 (every 3–4 days) is correct post-establishment guidance. The pamphlet's phase 2 transition to every 3–4 days is consistent with ISA, NC State Extension, and general professional practice for year-1 pachysandra maintenance after roots have begun establishing.
✓
Cool weather / moist soil exception is correctly applied. The pamphlet explicitly states to skip or reduce watering "during cool weather or in periods of frequent rainfall when the soil remains moist." This has been built into the Tier 1-Cool logic tier.
▲
Application method (overhead spray) remains a concern even with pamphlet frequency. The pamphlet does not specify method. Regardless of the watering frequency, overhead spray at 2–3× per week during warm weather significantly elevates Volutella blight risk for pachysandra. Soaker/drip at soil level is maintained as the required method throughout both phases. Source: NC State Extension — "overhead watering should be avoided"; AlmostGrass — "avoid watering overhead as fungus loves moisture."
✓
Lawn zones: daily watering replaced with weather-tiered M/W/F schedule. Virginia Cooperative Extension and turf research consistently recommend deep, infrequent watering (2–3 non-consecutive days/week) for established tall fescue on clay soils, using manual cycle-and-soak to avoid runoff on slopes. The previous daily-at-40% approach promoted shallow rooting and elevated brown patch risk. The new tiered scaling preserves calibrated 100% baseline run times (which already account for soil/slope realities) while adjusting to actual daily ET demand. Source: VA Coop. Ext. 430-010 — tall fescue irrigation guidelines; cycle-and-soak documentation from Rain Bird and Hunter Industries.
ℹ
Phase transition date: Jul 1, 2026 (84 days from April 8 installation — extended to 12-week establishment per ISA and pamphlet guidance). At that point the plant schedule automatically steps down to Phase 2: every 3–4 days normal, weekly in cool weather, daily in heat.
